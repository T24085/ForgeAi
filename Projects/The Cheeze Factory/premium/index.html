Our Factory

The Cheeze Factory began in 1978 when Ruth and Daniel Grayson converted an unused feed barn into a tiny production room and sold hand-cut wheels at county markets.

By 1989, the family built its first temperature-controlled cave, expanded to washed-rind and aged cheddar styles, and earned a reputation for long, patient aging.

Today, the second generation still runs daily make-room shifts, with the same rule posted over the vat: do not rush the curd.

Cheese Through Time

  • c. 5500 BCE

    Archaeological residues in pottery strainers from modern Poland show early cheese making among Neolithic farmers.

  • c. 3000-1000 BCE

    Cheese was made and traded in the ancient Near East and Mediterranean; Egyptian and Greek sources describe dairy curds and cheeses.

  • Middle Ages

    European monasteries refined many regional methods, helping preserve and spread distinctive cheese styles.

  • 1860s-1900s

    Pasteur's fermentation research and later milk pasteurization transformed safety and consistency, enabling modern large-scale production.
